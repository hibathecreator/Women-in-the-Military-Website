More Resources


For more information on this topic, visit the sources pageto see the websites the author used to make this website or explore the list below.

Online Sources For All The Organizations Discussed on This Website

https://www.nationalww2museum.org/war/articles/its-your-war-too-women-wwii
-Briefly discusses all of the women’s military organizations discussed on this website.

http://libcdm1.uncg.edu/cdm/search/collection/WVHP/searchterm/interviews/field/format/mode/all/conn/and/order/title
-A database filled with oral histories from veterans of WAC, WAAC, WAVES, WASP, and the ANC. Published by the Women Veterans Historical Project.

https://guides.library.harvard.edu/schlesinger/WWII
-Individual accounts of women in the military.

WAC

https://history.army.mil/books/wwii/Wac/
-A digitized copy of the book The Women's Army Corps by Mattie E. Treadwell. The book includes an extremely thorough history along with hundreds of photographs. The book was published by the Center of Military History United States Army in 1991.

https://www.armywomen.org/wacHistory.shtml
-A detailed history of WAAC and WAC by the Women's Army Corps Veterans' Association-Army Women United.

https://www.nytimes.com/2020/06/17/magazine/6888th-battalion-charity-adams.html
-A New York Times article about the first black female battalion in WAC and the part it played in World War II.

http://libcdm1.uncg.edu/cdm/search/collection/WVHP/field/descri/searchterm/WAAC
- WAC and WAAC Artifacts and oral histories collected by the Women Veterans Historical Project.

https://www.marshallfoundation.org/library/collection/subject-guides/womens-army-corps/#!/collection=927
-Women Army Corps Archive funded by the George C. Marshall Foundation. It contains collections of letters, notes, and diaries written by WAC members.

https://catalog.archives.gov/id/10476536
-The National Archives database for the WAC and WAAC.

https://ny.pbslearningmedia.org/resource/bf4e42ff-5707-49b8-910b-d0e5970fdcc3/helen-denton-womens-army-corps/
https://ny.pbslearningmedia.org/resource/ca6b7095-8d22-43a9-ad72-c0b6ffd2ae16/myrtle-edwards-tech-sergeant-wacs/
-Two full interviews(45 min) from WAC veterans.

WAVES

https://ww2db.com/other.php?other_id=24
-The World War II database. Provides an overview of the history of WAVES along with 158 photographs.

https://www.history.navy.mil/our-collections/photography/people---special-topics/women-in-the-navy/waves---wwii.html
-A large collection of WAVES photographs covering every part of their lives from recruitment and training to recreation time. Also includes individual WAVES portraits. Collection created by the Naval History and Heritage Command.

https://www.womenshistory.org/exhibits/waves-world-war-ii
-A detailed slideshow compiled by the National Women’s History Museum that includes an overview of WAVES history along with individual stories, audio records, and videos made during that time period.

https://www.usni.org/press/oral-histories/waves#anchor-7
-Includes brief biographies of numerous WAVES along with two audio clippings.

WASP

http://www.wwiihistoryclass.com/video/women/04_wasps.html
-A video in which WASP verteranS discuss their experiences and memories from their time during World War II.

https://www.womenshistory.org/exhibits/women-airforce-service-pilots-wasps-wwii
-A slideshow created by the National Women’s History Museum that includes an overview of WASP history along with individual stories, audio records, and videos made during that time period.

https://waspmuseum.org/
-A museum dedicated to WASP artifacts, albums, and photo collections. The website only displays a small portion of the collection.

https://www.nationalww2museum.org/war/articles/katherine-lanndeck-wwii-ferry-pilot
-An article by the National World War II Museum about the experiences of ferry pilot Teresa James and the dedicated service of WASP during the war.

https://airandspace.si.edu/stories/editorial/flying-homefront-women-airforce-service-pilots-wasp
-A brief history of WAFS, WFTD, and WASP. Created by the Smithsonian National Air and Space Museum.

https://abcnews.go.com/Politics/women-airforce-service-pilots-wwii-receive-congressional-gold/story?id=10057074
-An ABC news article from 2009 that discusses President’s Obama’s decision to give WASP veterans the Presidential Medal of Freedom and the events that led up to it.

ANC

https://history.amedd.army.mil/ancwebsite/anchome.html
-Gives detailed information about the efforts of the ANC from the Revolutionary war to present day. This website provides hundreds of photographs along with numerous oral history, documents, and videos.

https://ww2.fsu.edu/exhibits/online-exhibit
-An online exhibit by the Florida State University that uncovers individual stories of women in World War II.

https://youtu.be/iDExHl_aVQ8
-This video gives a descriptive history of the ANC along with individual accounts of nurses.

https://e-anca.org/
-The home page of the Army Nurse Corps Association where one can learn about the history of the ANC and learn about its current efforts.

https://history.army.mil/books/wwii/72-14/72-14.HTM
-A digitized book called The Army Nurse Corps: A Commemoration of World War II Service by Judith Bellafaire. Written in 1993.

http://www.wwiihistoryclass.com/video/women/10_medicalangels.html
-A video in which verteran World War II nurses discuss their challenges and responsibilities during the war. Video composed by Bristol Production.

SPAR

https://media.defense.gov/2017/Jul/02/2001772319/-1/-1/0/SPARS.PDF
-A digital copy of The Coast Guard & The Women’s Reserve in World War II by Robin J. Thomson. Published in 1992. It details the history of WASP, necessary qualities of candidates, and is complete with quotes and numerous photographs.

https://www.womenofwwii.com/coast-guard/
-Includes multiple SPAR photographs from the US Coast Guard collections.

https://www.womenshistory.org/exhibits/spars-coast-guard-women-wwii
-A slideshow created by the National Women’s History Museum that includes an overview of SPAR history along with individual stories, audio records, and videos made during that time period.

https://www.history.uscg.mil/Portals/1/FlorenceFinchHistory.pdf?ver=2019-05-22-065202-063
-Oral History Interview of SPAR Florence Finch conducted in 2007 by the United States Coast Guard.

https://media.defense.gov/2018/Jun/14/2001931622/-1/-1/0/KROLL_WRITERHARRIETSPAR.PDF
-Oral History Interview of SPAR Harriet Writer conducted in 2012 by the United States Coast Guard.
